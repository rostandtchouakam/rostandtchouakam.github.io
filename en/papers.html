Working Paper

Solving Consumption–Investment Models with Theory-Informed Neural Networks (TINNs)

Embedding economic structure into deep learning to solve high-dimensional dynamic programs. This paper explores how theory-informed neural network architectures can leverage known economic properties — such as concavity, homogeneity, and boundary conditions — to efficiently solve consumption–investment problems that are intractable with traditional numerical methods.
